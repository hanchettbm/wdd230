Preston History

Bear River Massacre Site

by Baden Hanchett

Preston Idaho is full of rich history. Here in this historic location was one of the most tragic meeting of two cultures that the state of Idaho has ever expirenced. At around 1:00am on January 29, 1863, some soldiers with all of their weponds moved to attack a group of 450 Shoshoni men, women, and children that had been living at the banks of the Bear River. The Shoshoni lost nearly 275 people due to this attack of the California Volunteers. now knowing the tragities that took place there is a monument dedicated to the Massacre just 4.5 miles, or 10 minutes, out of Preston. It is a wonderful place to vist to honor those that came before us. just make sure to stay updated onthe weather. For more information visit this website.
